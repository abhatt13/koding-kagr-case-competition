# KODING with KAGR Presentation Structure
## Wednesday Presentation - Story Flow

**Total Duration:** 10-12 minutes + Q&A
**Slides:** 8-10 slides

---

## Presentation Arc: "From Challenge to Opportunity"

### Story Flow
1. **Set the Stage** - What's the problem?
2. **Understand the Data** - What do we know?
3. **Identify the Gaps** - Where are we falling short?
4. **Propose Solutions** - How do we fix it?
5. **Show the Numbers** - Will it work?
6. **Address Concerns** - Implementation & flexibility

## SLIDE 6: Dynamic Pricing Implementation (1 minute)

### Visual
- Split visual:
  - **Left:** Heat map showing high-demand games (rivalry, tournaments) vs. low-demand (weekday, off-peak)
  - **Right:** Georgia Tech case study box: "$8.2M Year 1, 186% ROI"

### Content
**Current State:** Fixed pricing across all games

**The Problem:**
- Underpricing high-demand games (leaving money on table)
- Overpricing low-demand games (empty seats)

**The Solution: Sport-Specific Dynamic Pricing**
- Football: Rivalry games +20%, weekday games -15%
- Basketball: Conference games +15%, non-conference -10%
- Algorithm adjusts based on: opponent, day of week, weather, team performance

**Precedent:**
- 68% of Power 5 schools use dynamic pricing
- Georgia Tech: +$8.2M Year 1 (12.3% revenue increase)
- Penn State: +$386K per game average

**Financial Impact:** 12% ticket revenue increase = **$4.2M annually**

**Implementation Cost:** $800K-$1.2M (Ticketmaster/SeatGeek partnership)
**Payback Period:** 3-4 months

### What to Say
"Right now, we charge the same price whether it's a rivalry game on Saturday night or a weekday game against a bottom-tier opponent. That's leaving millions on the table.

68% of Power 5 schools have implemented dynamic pricing. Georgia Tech generated an additional $8.2 million in their first year - that's a 186% return on investment.

The key word here is SPORT-SPECIFIC. The football algorithm is different from basketball, which is different from volleyball. We're not just randomly changing prices - we're using data on opponent strength, day of week, historical attendance, even weather patterns.

Yes, there's an implementation cost - about $1 million. But Georgia Tech paid that back in 3-4 months. This is the fastest ROI initiative in our portfolio."

## Presentation Delivery Tips

### Body Language
- **Stand, don't sit** (if format allows)
- **Open posture** - no crossed arms
- **Move purposefully** - walk to the screen to point at charts
- **Eye contact** - scan the room, don't fixate on one judge
- **Hand gestures** - use them to emphasize key points

### Vocal Delivery
- **Pace:** Slightly slower than conversation (nervous tendency is to rush)
- **Pause:** After key statistics, let them land
- **Volume:** Project to back of room
- **Inflection:** Go UP on questions, DOWN on statements
- **Emphasis:** Bold words in script should be LOUDER

### Chart Presentation
- **Point physically** at the chart (don't just reference verbally)
- **Say what you're showing:** "Look at this bar..." "Notice the gap here..."
- **Numbers first, insight second:** "$4.4 million" then "Why? Because..."
- **Red = problems, Green = solutions** (use color coding verbally)

### Handling Questions
- **Pause 2 seconds** before answering (shows you're thinking)
- **Repeat the question** if clarification needed
- **Answer in 3 parts:** "Good question. [Acknowledge] Here's the answer. [Answer] Here's why it matters. [Context]"
- **If you don't know:** "Great question. I don't have that specific data in front of me, but my hypothesis would be... I'd want to validate that before committing to a number."

### Time Management
- **8 minutes:** Core presentation (Slides 1-10)
- **2-4 minutes:** Q&A buffer built into each slide
- **If running over:** Skip to Slide 10 (summary) and say "Happy to dive deeper on any specific initiative in Q&A"

## The Night Before

1. **Practice out loud** 3 times (time yourself)
2. **Record yourself** (check for verbal tics: "um," "like," "you know")
3. **Sleep 7-8 hours** (fatigue kills performance)
4. **Eat breakfast** (low blood sugar = brain fog)
5. **Arrive 15 minutes early** (test tech, calm nerves)

---

## Mindset

**Remember:**
- You know this material better than anyone in the room
- Judges WANT you to succeed (they're not adversarial)
- Nerves are energy - channel them into enthusiasm
- A 10-minute presentation is a conversation, not a performance
- They're evaluating your thinking, not your polish

**If you mess up:**
- Keep going (they won't notice)
- Don't apologize ("Sorry, I'm nervous" draws attention)
- If you lose your place: "Let me highlight the key takeaway here..." (bridges to next point)

---

- **Thank the judges** (eye contact, genuine)
